"What do you mean we can't get her?" she cried, grabbing his arm. "We have to!"

"Look, Bessie..." I just stared at her.

Her eyes widened. "What? What is it you're not telling me?"

I exhaled. I could barely get the words out. "They're, ah, cooking her."

First she blanched, a look of horror on her face. Then she sprang from the carriage and burst through the kitchen door, knocking over everything in her wake, including a toddling boy.

Astonished by the noise,Mrs. Beck turned from the pot and faced Bessie who grabbed her by her dress skirt and screamed at her, "Are you mad? How can you do such a thing?"
