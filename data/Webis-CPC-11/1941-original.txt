Verax escaped in the same way, while
some boats put in to fetch Tutor and Classicus.

Even now the Roman fleet had not joined the army. They had, indeed, received orders, but fear
held them back, and the rowers were employed on various duties elsewhere. It must be admitted,
also, that Cerialis did not give them time enough to carry out his orders. He was a man of
sudden resolves and brilliant successes. Even when his strategy had failed, good luck always
came to his rescue.
