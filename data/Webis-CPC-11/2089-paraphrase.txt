The story of their horrible journey is unwritten, but it is known that many died of slow hunger and fatigue along the route, which led through swamps and thickets, with deep rivers barring their path; and not until the last of December did they reach the forts, after having been twelve weeks in the wilderness. The number of those who decomposed in the wreck or died on the journey is not recorded, but it was as large as to time petitions to the government--an unusual proceeding at that early date.
