Not long before this time he had returned from Caen, in Normandy, and he advised me that when there he had studied the ground on which William the Conqueror had procured soldiers renown before he made his drop on England, and his resolution was that that Conqueror was surprisingly well instructed for his time in the art of war. He articulated his stated intent to draft on this subject; but many events before long later called him to India, which became the outlook of his own mastery in soldiers and civilised command.

T. F.

To Come (Vol. viii., p. 468.).--In the Lower Saxon dialect, to draw close is camen, and the imperfect, as in Gothic, quam. It would consequently look like that the English came is not an research, but a partial restoration or preservation of a very aged form.
