Zanchino Ugolini, thee canonist, says that he was at an immoral priests, condemnation. He was punished by his questioners not for his licentiousness, but he was at Mass everyday in sin, and stated that he pardoned himself for putting on sacred vestments.

[1] Tractat de Haeret., cap.ii.

The Jews were never considered heretics. But the usury they commonly practiced gave away an unorthodox doctrine of theft, which made them liable under suspicion of heresy. We find several popes upbraiding them "for maintaining that usury is not sin." Some Christians also erred in that way and therefore became subject to questioning.
