My babies, there is a God, who hears and sees you.' 'A God, mau-mau! Where does he live?' asked the babies. 'He lives in the sky,' he replied; 'and when you are beaten, or cruelly treated, or fall in to any trouble, you must ask help of him, and he will always listen to and help you.' He taught them to kneel and say the Lord's Prayer. He implored them to refrain from lying and stealing, and to strive to obey their masters.

In the evening, when her mother's work was done, he would sit down under the sparkling vault of heaven, and calling her babies to her, would talk to them of the only Being that could effectually aid or protect them.
