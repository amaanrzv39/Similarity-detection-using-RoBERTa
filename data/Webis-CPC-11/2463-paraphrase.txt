Soon after the Korea's arrival in port, on the voyage in which they are interested, I visited the ship to interview the Chinese women on board, and there for the first time met our tiny dark-eyed mate, Kum Ping. They had been carefully coached on the way as to the visits they might get from foreign missionaries, and the replies to all our questions showed a guarded suspicion that seemed hopeless. Our cheerful interpreter talked on, nevertheless, and finally won a calm smirk and the offer of some roast duck (a great delicacy among Chinese).
