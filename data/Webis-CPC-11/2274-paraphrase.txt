"He, by great industry, made it manifest that there is any plant existing in the known world, that won't, with proper care, prosper in our climate." In a visit made by Sir W. Watson & Dr. Mitchell to Tradescant's garden in 1749, an account of which is inserted in the Philosophical Transactions, vol. xlvi. p. 160., it appears that it had been lots of years neglected, & the house belonging to it empty & ruined; but though the garden was covered with weeds, there remained among them manifest footsteps of its founder.
