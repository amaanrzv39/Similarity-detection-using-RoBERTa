
It therefore appears to me incontestably true, that not only governments did not begin by arbitrary
power, which is but the corruption and extreme term of government, and at length brings it
back to the law of the strongest, against which governments were at first the remedy, but even
that, allowing they had commenced in this manner, such power being illegal in itself could
never have served as a foundation to the rights of society, nor of course to the inequality
of institution.
