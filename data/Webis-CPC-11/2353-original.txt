Many interesting topics are touched upon--among
which we point to his remarks on the difficulties experienced by him in meeting the literary
requirements of the day, and the peculiar demands of editors; his opinion of Mr. Carlyle; the
present condition of the stage, the absurd pretensions of actors, and the delusions attempted
respecting the "legitimate" drama; the question of the laureateship, and his own qualifications
for holding that office; his habits of reading; and finally an avowal of his religious opinions.
