
There were now eight battleships, all pre-dreadnoughts, left at Tenedos, and at noon six of
them started off in line a-head toward the strait. The English ships already within were passing
further up and went out of sight.

The bombarding ships were steaming constantly up and down, turning at each end of the stretch,
which is about a couple of miles long.

A long thin veil of black smoke was drifting slowly westward from the fighting. At about 1:30
Erenkeui Village, standing high on the Asiatic side, received a couple of shells.
