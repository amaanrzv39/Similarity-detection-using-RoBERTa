From a household book of the Earl of Northumberland, it appears that his relatives, during the winter, fed mostly on salt meat & salt fish, with "an appointment of 160 gallons of mustard." On flesh days through the year, breakfast for my lord & lady was a loaf of bread, five manchets, a quart of beer, a quart of wine, half a chine of mutton, or a chine of beef, boiled.
