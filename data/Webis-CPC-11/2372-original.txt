
He urged the erection as soon as possible of a chapel, which should be of dimensions suitable
for the demands of the college.  There were other objects calling for a far greater outlay
of money than the resources of the college afforded, but he deemed this of great importance,
and succeeded in getting appropriations for it first.  He hastened the selection of the site
and the drawing of the plans.  the completion of the work was much retarded owing to the want
of funds, but his interest in its erection never flagged.
