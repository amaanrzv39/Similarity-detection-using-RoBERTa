Now, even though it is regretful that we don't see better homesteads and better fences in Ireland, we still cannot concede that the tenant's being obliged in keeping it in repair can be of any difficulty, as he lays out barely anything on them: he does not even work on the improvement; and his time which may be busy, is wasted in idleness, in swelling the train, or cheering the sentiments, of some mercenary pest.
