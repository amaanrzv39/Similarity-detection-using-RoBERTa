John George II., 'the father of his citizens,' was not remiss in kind for the mountain metropolis. He sent Lieutenant-Colonel George Hermann von Schweinitz, a courageous and skilled leader, with three companies of infantry and one of dragoons, to carry out the resistance. These troops mustered merely two hundred and ninety men all told; however this small crew, aided by the people, magnificently seized at bay for two long months a complete Swedish military of eight brigades, with a hundred and nine pieces of artillery.
