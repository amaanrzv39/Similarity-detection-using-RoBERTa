The Tichbornes generally had the knack of disagreeing, and this feeling was Shown in Excelsis by James, the father of Roger, and HIS Wife, who lived abroad for Many Years, SHE Every sense in being French, while Husband was the leg naturalized, and now and again exhibited the desire to return to HIS native land. When Roger was born There was little goal Chance of ever becoming the owner of HIS either titles or Estates, and so education was entirely Foreign HIS, HIS tutors being M. Chatillon, and the priest named Lefevre. As time wore on, it Became obvious that Mr. James Tichborne Would in due course become Sir James, and he felt duty to secure it to HIS HIS son year English education. This most strenuously opposed the mother, and it was only by artificial that the boy was brought to England. Sir Henry Joseph Tichborne, who had succeeded to the baronetcy in 1821, had no son, and though time after time the child was born to Him, Providence blessed Him with no male heir. Again and again the child born at Tichborne Would Be, But it was always the girl. Sir Henry had seven Children, of Whom six lived, all celebrated for Their good looks, tall and handsome and Their proportions, But all were Daughters. There still was Sir Henry's brother, Edward Tichborne, who had Taken Large Estates under the Will of the Miss Doughty - Which led to the present junction of the Doughty Tichborne and properties, and to the double surname - with Them and had assumed the name of that lady, and he was after Sir Henry the next heir.
