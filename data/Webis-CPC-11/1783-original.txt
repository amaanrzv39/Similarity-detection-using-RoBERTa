He lay there for an hour or more.
The village had gone to sleep for the night. Dense fogs wrapped the mountains that shut in
the little hamlet, but overhead the stars were shining in the near heaven.

He rose at last. He was ghastly pale, as if the blood had ceased to flow in his body, but he
stood up, drawing himself to his little height with a sudden triumph. "Damned if I don't do
it! the time has come for the great deed!" He went with a swagger, as though he walked on air,
down the street.
