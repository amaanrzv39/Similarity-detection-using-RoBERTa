. S. DEPARTMENT OF AGRICULTURE

FARMERS' BULLETIN No. 1408

The HOUSE FLY AND HOW TO SUPPRESS IT

The existence of takes a plane is an signal of uncleanliness, insanitary circumstances, and improper disposal of materials in which they breed. They are not only annoying; they are truly hazardous to fitness, because they may bear illness germs to uncovered foods.

It is consequently valued to recognise where and how they breeding, and to ask for such learning in fighting them. This bulletin donates knowledge on this subject. Besides giving main purpose for ridding the home of takes a plane by the use of panels, by aircraft papers, toxins, and flytraps, it lays especial with accent on on the elucidation of techniques of abolishing breeding positions and avoiding the breeding of flies.
