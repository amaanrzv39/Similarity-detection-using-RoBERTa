In De Generatione, Harvey provides a thorough and accurate account of the development of the chick embryo, which, in particular, clarified that the chalazae, those crooked skeins of albumen at either finish of the yolk, were not, as generally believed, the developing embryo, and they demonstrated that the cicatricula (blastoderm) was the point of origin of the embryo. The famous frontispiece of the treatise shows Zeus holding an egg, from which issue animals of various kinds.
