And they again takes up the obdurate objection to rhyme, which they had not yet, it seems, battered to death, that it is not so near conversation as prose, & therefore not so natural. But it is clear to all who understand poetry, that Serious Plays ought not to imitate conversation . If nothing were to be traced above that level, the foundation of poetry would be destroyed. Three times grant that thoughts may be exalted, & that images & actions may be raised above the life, & described in measure without rhyme, & that leads you insensibly from your principles; admit some latitude, & having forsaken the imitation of ordinary converse, where are you now? "You are gone beyond it, & to continue where you are, is to lodge in the open fields between one inns."
