But as a mere cottage roof, it cannot be sublime, and whatever sublimity it derives from the wildness or sternness which the hills   possess granted it within its cap, is, so far forth, parasitical. The  hill itself would possess been grand, which is much many than picturesque; but the cottage cannot be grand as such, and the parasitical grandeur which it may possess  via  accidental qualities, is the spirit for which men possess long accepted consumption the inaccurate word "Picturesque."
