His little fund had dwindled. He had,
as he thought, barely enough to conduct his business to the time when he could legally have
an auction. But how was he to do this and pay his rent? That problem troubled him. It was finally
solved by the consent of his landlord, in consideration of a high rate of interest, to wait
for his rent until Rumble had his auction. When this arrangement was made, the pawnbroker,
who had been gloomy for some time, again wore a cheerful look. His daughter had advised him
to pay his rent and curtail his business for the time being; but that, he said, would never
do; and when he had tided over the crisis in his affairs, he went on distributing his money
among the people who brought him their old clothes and their all but worthless jewellery.
