
She stood within the counter, her hands clasped behind her back, her shoulders pressed against
the wall, her feet braced out.  Her face was bright with the wind and her own thoughts; as
a fire in a similar day of tempest glows and brightens on a hearth, so she seemed to glow,
standing there, and to breathe out energy.  It was the first time Ballantrae had visited that
wine-seller's, the first time he had seen the wife; and his eyes were true to her.
