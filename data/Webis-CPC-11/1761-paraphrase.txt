"The little that occured can be told in a short span of time." says W. S. Merriwether, the New York Herald correspondent. "It is understood that one moment was a brightly smiling city and another a hellish display of swirling flames. 30,000 people are believed to have perished. One minute the church bells were playing jubilantly in St. Pierre's and the next a sobbing lament from its unfortunate inhabitants."
