"And if you were not, Max, I ought to disown you," they whispered.

The strains of a waltz mingled with talk & laughter floated out of the open window. His voice dropped to a low intensity. "We are getting ready in Company A," they said; "the traitors will be dropped. They are getting ready to fight for Missouri & for the South."
The girl felt his excitement, his exaltation.

They leaned forward until his face was close to hers.

"And now?" they said.

They sank back heavily in to the corner.

"I am ready to work, to starve, to go to prison, to help--"

"All?" they repeated. "Oh, if a woman could only do more!"

"Is that all, Jinny?"

Virginia straightened.

"And is there nothing--for me?"

"Are you doing this for a reward?" they demanded.
