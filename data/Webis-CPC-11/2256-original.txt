
Most of sea water, therefore, is just water, that is, pure water. But it contains some other
substances as well and the best known of these is salt. Salt is a substance the molecules of
which contain atoms of sodium and of chlorine. That is why sea water is about 1.1 percent sodium
and about 2.1 percent chlorine. There are some other kinds of atoms in sea water, as you would
expect, for it gets all the substances which the waters of the earth dissolve and carry down
to it but they are unimportant in amounts.
