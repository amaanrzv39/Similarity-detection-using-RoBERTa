From 2 till 4 is the traditional time of excercise, the most usual modes of which are walking and rowing. Cricketing, and all games of ball, are much practised in their respective seasons. Towards 4 P.M, they begin to flock in for dinner. A Latin grace is read by two of the dons, and forthwith the demolition of eatable proceeds. Though there is a common hall, there is no common table. On the contrary, there is no end to the vcariety, both as respects rank, provision, and priviliage. Hall lasts about as respects rank, provision, and priviliage. Hall lasts about three-quarters of an hour. Two scholars conclude the business by reading a long Latin grace - the dons, it is said, being too full after dinner for such duty. After hall is emphatically lounging time.
