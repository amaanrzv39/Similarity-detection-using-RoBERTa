St. Pierre was a vast charnel-house. Skirting for nearly a league the blue waters of the Caribbean,
its smoking ruins became the funeral pyre of 30,000, not one of whom lived long enough to tell
adequately a story that will stand grim, awful, unforgotten as that of Herculaneum, when the
world is older by a thousand years.
