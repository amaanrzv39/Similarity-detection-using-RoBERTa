
Meantime, general acceptance being given the histological scheme of Gerlach, according to which
the mass of the white substance of the brain is a mesh-work of intercellular fibrils, a proximal
idea seemed attainable of the way in which the ganglionic activities are correlated, and, through
association, built up, so to speak, into the higher mental processes. Such a conception accorded
beautifully with the ideas of the associationists, who had now become dominant in psychology.
