He came with James on the way back, staying silent for most of the time. To him, it didn't feel like Henry was dead, instead it seemed like a nightmare that he needed to wake up from.  The vision of his mother was agonizing and caused tears to fall from his eyes.  Looking back on his childhood caused pain, but it was necessary to help repair the anguish he had experienced.

THE MEETING.
