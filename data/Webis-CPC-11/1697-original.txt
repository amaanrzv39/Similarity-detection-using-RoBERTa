There was
a third question, concerning the fisheries on the Great Banks of Newfoundland. As to these,
the general right of all nations to frequent the Banks, being open sea, was explicitly admitted;
but the subjects of a foreign state had no right to fish within the maritime jurisdiction of
Great Britain, much less to land with their catch on coasts belonging to her. The provisions
of the Treaty of 1783 therefore would not be renewed, unless for an equivalent.
