"The New York Herald has stimulated the study of the weather for many years, and its managers now send word to England by the Atlantic cable when a strom is to evaluated there. They have lately sent notice of so many ugly ones, which have directly arrived, that our English cousins are accusing of the unfair treatment of the Herald."

"Are they really so logical?" asked Jack.

"Yes," said the Professor, "they jokingly intimate that when Providence controlled the weather they fared well enough; but that since the Herald has undertaken to run that department they have been dooomed to storms, fogs, and rain. To give an instance of the faith, Jack that the English people put in our Signal Service, there is a story told of an English lady who last autumn desired to give a lawn party.
