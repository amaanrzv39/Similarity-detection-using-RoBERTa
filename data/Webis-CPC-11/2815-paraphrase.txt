He took her and raised up in the air; and she was trembling like anything and shivering. He held her a moment; she protested with sorry, and he let them go for ever to any where.

"Farewell, Agnes!" he said. "I am going. Never we see again."

She raised both her hands and pressed the sharp cross to her bosom, but made no answer. 

"Your wish will be done," he continued. "Immediately when I leave you, your grandmother will come to you, and the people who brought you here will through away you to the high-road. For me, since it is your will, I part here. Farewell, Agnes!"
