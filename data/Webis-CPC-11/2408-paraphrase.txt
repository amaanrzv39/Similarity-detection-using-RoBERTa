See account in D. N. B. Bramble was mayor in 1377, 1383-4-5. He was the political leader of the group of ruler Richard's friends in London. Of his public career I shall not treat since that is sufficiently covered elsewhere. To illustrate his financial dealings, the following abstracts of documents are significant. In September 1377, the King on loan L10, 000 of Bramble, Walworth, Philpot and John Adele (grocer, later Mayor of London), and sure other merchants, for whom these were attorneys, pledge the crown jewels.
