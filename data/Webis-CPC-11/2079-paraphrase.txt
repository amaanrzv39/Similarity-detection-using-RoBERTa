"There it is," scribbles a precursor to his friend, "that we promote sentences of divine law, like precise arrows to harm the enemy. Then, we take the shields of righteousness, the helmet of salvation, faith's armour and the sword of the Spirit, which is the Word of God." With a magnificent end in sight Reculfus of Soissons required his clergy to have a missal, a sermon, the Gospels, a study of martyrs, an antiphonary, a psalter, a forty homily book of Gregory, and as many Christian books they could collect.
