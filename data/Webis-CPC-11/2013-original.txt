
These are the narratives as compiled from authorities most vague and diverse, and yet, when
taken together, most indisputable. At that time, before the Revolution, when, save a narrow
belt on the Atlantic coast, the whole country was a forest, authentic news was rare, and records
carelessly kept, if kept at all. Soldiers marching hundreds of miles through a wilderness had
no time to compose elaborate journals, and had something else to think of than the curiosity
of posterity.
