The Exhibition has surplanted more common places of amusement, such as the theater, all of which remain in business, though operating at a loss.  Visitors spend very little and are noted by the shopkeepers to be unexciting.  There is considerable worry and stress that the price of admission may soon drop to a shilling and measures have been taken to prevent such a calamity.  However, most of this fear remains unfounded and kept in exaggeration.
