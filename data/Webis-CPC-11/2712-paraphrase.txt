To be sure we have had Bancroft, and Sparks, and Hildreth. But their brethren have told us as little about the history of the people as Lingard, Hume, Hallam, and all the rest of them told England.

Shall we, then, let the students of posterity remain in the dark on such questions as these. Why Providence became the second city of New England. Why she left Newport so badly in the race for prosperity. Why Buffalo and Cincinnati went up, while Black Rock and North Bend went down. 

Why Chicago became the largest manufacturing city on the continent Why New England kept the town-meeting, and the West preferred the township and the county. Why a thousand and one other important things happened.
