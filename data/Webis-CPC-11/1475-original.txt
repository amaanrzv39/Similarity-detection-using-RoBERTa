A laugh raised at
the expense of a brother is enough to justify the severest slash. Comparative Philology, which
owes its existence and progress to the labors of German scholars, and whose first representative,
Bopp, is still living and teaching in Berlin, is more and more pursued of late. Sanscrit is
now taught universally; and lectures are delivered on the affinities of the Indo-Germanic languages
with each other and with the mother-tongue of all.
