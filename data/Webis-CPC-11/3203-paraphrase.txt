Mr Idle must admit that he was coming to the conclusion on distinct and logical grounds. After viewing, to his relief, and with many necessary intervals of repose, the placid, spectacle of his past life, he made a discovery that all of the great disasters which had tested him in earlier lives had been caused by him letting himself be decieved into imitating some example of activity and industry that had been set by others. The trials he refers to were three, and may be reckoned up. First the disaster of being an outcast in school, second, the disaster of severe illness, and third, the disaster of befriending a great bore.
