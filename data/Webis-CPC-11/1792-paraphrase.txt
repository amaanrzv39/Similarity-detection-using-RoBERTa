But the mind was as clearly the result of the bodily association as the music of the harpsichord is the result of the instrumental mechanism. The mind shared the decrepitude of the body in extreme old age, and in the full vigor of youth a sudden injury to the brain might forever destroy the intellect of a Plato or a Shakespeare. But the third principle,--the soul,--the something lodged within the body, which yet was to stay alive it? Where was that soul unseen out of the ken of the anatomist?
