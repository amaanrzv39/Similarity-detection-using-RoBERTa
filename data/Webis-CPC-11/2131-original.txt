Happily their sufferings were of short duration, for the enemy, in the fulfilment of a barbarous
usage, cut off their heads as they fell, as trophies of their own personal prowess.

Sir Charles McCarthy saw that the day was lost, and that it would be but an inglorious sacrifice
of his own staff, and the few soldiers that yet remained, to continue on the field. He, therefore,
prepared to retire; but this resolution--which, in the breast of so brave an officer, was slow
to find a place--was taken too late.
