
These concoctions were publicly sold at Rome, their ingredients consisting of herbs of various
kinds, in the culling and testing of which the shepherds were often employed. The remora, or
sucking-fish, certain bones of the frog, the astroit, or star-fish, and the hippomanes were
also used. Horace informs us that dried human marrow and liver were also had recourse to:

  "Exsucta uti medulla et aridum jecur
  Amoris esset poculum."[103]

  That his parch'd marrow might compose,
  Together with his liver dried, an amorous dose.
