Whomever he is able to pierce with all his arrows, he should have them muted, still, quiet, lying immobile, lither and full of ease.  When Theophrastus was asked, in conformation, what kind of beast or thing he thought a playful, unjustifiable love to be? He replied, that is was a passion of inactive and lethargic spirits. [224] When he defined lechery, from which pretty description of tickling-tricks, that of Diogenes, the Cynic, was not very inconsistent.
