He has much of respect as asserted by the initial sense of it, which amidst the ancients, Gellius states, signified injury. His prosperity was larger than his mind could accept, and he is intoxicated with it; and if he should take a nap as long as Epimenides or the Seven Sleepers he would not ever be sober again. He took his degree and went forward lord by mandamus, without accomplishing workouts of merit. His honour's but an immunity from worth, and his nobility a dispensation for doing things ignoble. He anticipates that men's hats should go by plane off before him like a gale, and not presume to stand in the way of his outlook, which is habitually over their heads. All the benefit he has is but to proceed before or sit before, in which his nether components take location of his top, that extend still, in evaluation, but commoners.
