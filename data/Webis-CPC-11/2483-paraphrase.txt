We gave him a tube of quinine for his next attack of malaria. Once they caught us there would be little left for us.We shall never reach Paris, we shall be here for ever and ever.

The longer we rested in San Giovanni the more hopeless seemed the chance of getting away from it. The Serbian Government was close on our heels.   That evening we were sitting with the Frenchmen, it was Monday. They were depressed. 


Said Jan, rashly, "I think we ought to be home in a week."

Dum put on the superior French air, which is aggravating even in a nice man.

They talked each other for a few minutes.
This lent a new interest to life.
