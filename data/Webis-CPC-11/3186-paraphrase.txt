Browne tells us, "you can do a good deed but you don't have to do it exclusively, or often."  Browne published his study of the reactions of eggs exposed to physical and chemical stimuli in an effort to document the reactions of the embryos and any developmental anomalies.  His findings were met with mixed feelings by his contemporaries, who adhered to mainstream ideas about the subject and were not willing to deviate from the standard conceptions of the times.
