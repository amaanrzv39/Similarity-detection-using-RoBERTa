To check his next attack of malaria, though, we gave him a tube of quinine.

With the long rest in San Giovanni, it began to seem impossible to get away from it. If caught by the ever closing in Serbian government, there would be little left for us. That monday evening we were sitting with the frenchmen, who, too, were depressed, and at last Tweedledum spoke-

"We shall be here forever, Paris is a dream."

"Oh," said Jan, unwittingly. "I think we shall be home in about a week."

Dum put on the elite French air, which is debasing even in a good man.

"Vous croyez?" he said.

"I'm sure," said Jan.

"A dinner," replied Dum.

"Good," said Jan.

This offered a new buzz to life.
