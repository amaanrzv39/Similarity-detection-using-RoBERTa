
He started off once more, this time without stopping to think where Hetty would be likely to
go, only rushing about in a sort of desperate way, calling her by name, and shouting for Cham.

He stopped on top of a high hill called the Ledge, and looked down the steep side of it a moment.
Hark! He certainly heard the whine of a dog. He clambered down a little way, and called his
loudest. The dog's whine answered him again. With a new hope in his heart, he called, and listened
until the whine grew louder and louder, and he recognized Cham's bark.
