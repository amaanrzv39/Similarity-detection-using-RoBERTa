The educational success of its contending section of the State should so far surpass that of its own, was something which the Western Shore could not tolerate.  The sections were given equal importance, in the early days of the State.  

St. John’s College was charted in the year 1784, which was two years after the establishment of Washington College.  [7] Annapolis was the place where it was to be founded and the old county Academy, “King William’s School,’ which was founded eighty years before was merged with it.  The two colleges were united in the University of Maryland, by the same act.  After the English type, it was modeled; its chancellor was the governor, and “Convocation of the University of Maryland” was to be the governing body.
