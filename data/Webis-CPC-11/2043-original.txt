And yet--and what an idea does the fact present of the
multitudinous resources, the unrivalled industry, the latent power of this country!--all that
heap of precious metals, all that is besides in circulation, with the addition of the bank-note
currency, is comparatively nothing when weighed against the true and real exchangeable wealth
of Great Britain; wealth of which this coined and convertible paper-money is merely the standard
sign of value, the recognised medium by which all things are bartered.
