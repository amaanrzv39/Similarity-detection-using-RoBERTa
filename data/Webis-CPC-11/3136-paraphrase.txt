He left the mansion to inhabit in a distant suburb, in a tiny home paid for a long time before, and conveyed to his wife. He inhabited with her in this flee, enjoying in the household happiness. The most painstaking training of his only girl child, Madame the Baroness of Hoüchters-leöen, who is no longer residing, the cultivation of his planted area, the public intercourse of numerous educated and estimable men, were his occupations and his pleasures.
About two years after the death of Prince Wenceslas of Lichtenstein, his kin and heir, the Prince Francis, observed Angelo in the street. He ranked his carriage to be finished, had him move into it, and advised him that, being sold on of his innocence, he was answered to make alters for the injustice of his uncle.
