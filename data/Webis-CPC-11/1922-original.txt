
Soaped, napkined and generally extinguished, I could only stammer, "You here in Brussels? What
a droll meeting!"

"Wherefore droll?" asked Joliet, with a huge surprise, which lasted him all through his next
sentence. "I come here to marry my daughter. Everything is ready; we count on your presence
at the wedding; the lawyer has drawn up the contract; and the breakfast is now cooking at the
best restaurant in the place."

"Francine's wedding, my dear Joliet!" I exclaimed.
