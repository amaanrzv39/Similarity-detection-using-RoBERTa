Cesar. ¶ But our histores farre differ from this, affirming that Cesar comming the second time,
was by the Britains with valiancie and martiall prowesse beaten and repelled, as he was at
the first, and speciallie by meanes that Cassibellane had pight in the Thames great piles of
trées piked with yron, through which his ships being entred the riuer, were perished and lost.
And after his comming a land, he was vanquished in battell, and constrained to flee into Gallia
with those ships that remained.
