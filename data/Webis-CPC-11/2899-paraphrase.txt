Be righteous and abide by the golden rule, my friends, if you are not anxious to see glory from this deck: Don’t touch, don’t go near the tree of grave; beware of the dog called hot biscuits; take care of the grease and the stomach will take care of itself.  My beloved brothers, the truth is, I have been a first chop dyspeptic for the best part of my life, and I am pretty well posted in what I am talking about. What I don’t know on this subject are not worth knowing.”
III.--RECITATIVE 
It can be never understood, the distance the Martyr’s appeal might have reached, since the extent of his great argument was cut short at this point by the appearance of the Pontifex Maximus in person on the stage of action.  For the coming sacrifice, the doomed victims were prepared to be ready.
