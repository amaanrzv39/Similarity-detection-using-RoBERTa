With these followers he and Hamet marched across the desert toward Derne, in the
kingdom of Tripoli. Eaton had not lost his boyish love of adventure yet, you see. This was
just one of the bold, daring undertakings that he may have dreamed of in those early days when
he stole away from his work to read with eager delight stories of wild venture and perilous
escape in the peaceful shades of the forest around Woodstock. Doubtless these desert marches
now entered upon far exceeded all his young imagination had pictured them.
