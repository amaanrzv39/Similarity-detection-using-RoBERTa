The main body drawn up in a long line remained inactie with the artillery, and a smaller crops as a rear-guard seemed destined to communicate with the fleet of Lord Cochrane at Cape Kolias. At the Piraeus, about 700 men were scattered about in all the disorder of an Eastern encampment, without making the slightest attempt to distract the attention of the Trukish troops.  The French General Gueheneuc and the Bavarian General Heideck, both witnessed the battle.
