
"On entering, the spectator is struck with the view of a bed of simple materials, and which
was pillaged by the Austrians. Hung round the room are the portraits of Frederick, of Catharine,
of Lekain--one of Voltaire himself, taken at the age of forty, and full of expression, with
a number of silhouettes of the celebrated men of the day.

"The window of this apartment looks upon the gardens, and upon a little wood, which has undergone
many changes since the death of Voltaire. Time however has hitherto respected a long and thick
row of elm trees, whither he was wont to repair at sunrise, and where he usually meditated
and recited aloud the scenes of his tragedies when finished, to any one whom he could find.
