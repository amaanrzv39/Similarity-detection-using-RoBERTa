They were
strong enough to rise once in their might and say they would not have slavery among them. But
in the petty details of every day, in their ordinary talk, and in their routine legislation,
their sympathies were still with the slave-holders. They would not enlist with them, but they
would fight their battles in their own way.

Their readiness to do what came to be called later, in a famous speech, the "dirty work" of
the South was seen in the tragic death of Rev. Elijah P. Lovejoy, in this very year of 1837.
