What do people call him?"

"A warbler.  He's called that because he possesses so little a voice." I said.

"His song might improve if he didn't toil so hard catching flies," said the second beggar.

Sighing and wiping the corner of his sinistral eye, the fourth beggar said, "Poor flies!"  He was known to have a tender-hearted side.

The third beggar declared, "I bet you anything he doesn't catch the next one.  I bet you a hundred to ten."

"Done, then," exclaimed the others.  But before the stakes could be counted out, the bird had already flown off.
