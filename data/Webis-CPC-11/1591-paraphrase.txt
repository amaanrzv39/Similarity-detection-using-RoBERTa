These, built on the same plan as ours are, but with flat bottoms, belonged to traders, and were solely adapted to the transport of merchandise. The stern, fancifully ornamented, rises two or three stages above the deck, and is the seat of the salesman. The inside of the both is filled with goods, and thatched over, leaving sufficient room underneath to accommodate two or three families--men, women, and children--who promiscuously take up their abode there.
