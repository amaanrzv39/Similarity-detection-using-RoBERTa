"It is, Seigneur," answered the Clerk, doffing his hat.

"Then hurl off your doublet and let us try a drop or two," said Guérande, with a wicked gaze at Annaïk which was not lost upon her lover.

"Saving your grace, I may not wrestle with you," said the Clerk, "for you are a polite man and I am nobody. You are the child of a lord and I am the child of a peasant."

"Ha! what! The child of a peasant, state you, and you take your alternative of the attractive young women of the village?"

"Seigneur, pardon me. I did not select this maiden; God provided her to me."
