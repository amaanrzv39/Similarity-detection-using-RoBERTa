’Tis true indeed, in many things they doe but trifle, but for the maine scope of those discourses,
’tis as manifest they seriously meant it, as any indifferent Reader may easily discerne; otherwise
sure Campanella (a man as well acquainted with his opinion, and perhaps his person as Cæsar
was) would never have writ an apologie for him.
