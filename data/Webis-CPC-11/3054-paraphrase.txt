Such a virtue could not be vegetable in its nature, but was, connected with metals. He pointed to the approved medicinal values which inhered in gems. Metals also had great medicinal ability. Antimony, lead, iron, mercury, were well known, and of special importance was copper, the Venus of the early chemists.

The medicinal value which inhered in Butler's stone and in other strong fermental remedies, van Helmont designated as "drif," which he said means, in the vernacular, sand or earth. This value requires a metallic body in which to inhere. The general concept is not unfamiliar, of a value or power or ferment which was attached to an object, and it is this type of explanation which was so preponderant in, Porta's Natural Magick.
