
"And you would be quite right not to do so, Monseigneur," said La Ramée. "I confess that, when
I am hungry, I know no pleasure equal to that of sitting down to a good dinner; and when I
remember that my Amphitryon is the grandson of Henry the Fourth, the pleasure is at least doubled
by the honour done to me."

The duke bowed. "My dear La Ramée," said he, "you are unequaled in the art of paying compliments."

"It is no compliment, Monseigneur," said La Ramée; "I say exactly what I think."
