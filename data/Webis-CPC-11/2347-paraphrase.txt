He travled to Cheltenham and spoke with Boisragon about his nerves. He was advised a treatment of the waters, and horse exercise. 
The offspring of the weeper, thought he had already "too much water;"  however he hired a nag. He took a small suburban lodge, and as nobody spoke to him, nor seemed to care about him, he grew better, and felt happier. This blest seclusion, "the world forgetting, by the world forgot," was not the fate of Sighmon: odd circumstances always brought him into notice.
