In like manner, in the lay schools, at Sainte-Barbe for example,[6120] every professor, private tutor, or even common superintendent, must be provided with a special authorization by the University. 

Staff and discipline, good teaching, every detail of study and recreation,[6121] all are imposed, conducted and restrained in these so-called free establishments; whatever they may be, ecclesiastic or secular, not only does the University surround and hamper them, but again it absorbs and assimilates them; it does not even leave them any external distinctive appearance.
