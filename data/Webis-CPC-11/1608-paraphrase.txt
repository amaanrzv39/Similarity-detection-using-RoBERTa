1861. 
A very attractive letter from HENRY LAURENS, second President of the Continental Congress, to his son, Col. JOHN LAURENS, dated Charleston, S.C., Aug. 14, 1776, now first available from the unique letter. It contains a vehement plea for Emancipation, and speaks with bitter contempt of England for hopeful the slave-trade in America. 
THE REBELLION; ITS LATENT CAUSES AND TRUE SIGNIFICANCE. In a Letter to his Friend who is in abroad. Henry T. Tuckerman. New York: Jas. G. Gregory. 1861.
