Their wives are along with them, primary their donkeys with "baskets which they expect to bear away full." ("Archives Nationales," F7, 3523. Letter of the municipal body of Lunel, November 4, 1791.) At Uzès it is with many annoyance that they can clear themselves of the peasants who came in to move ahead out the Catholic royalists. In vain "were they bestowed abundance to devour and to drink;" they depart away "in horrid comedy, principally the women who commanded the mules and asses to bear away the booty, and who had not foretold revisiting household with empty hands."
