Now Mr. Davis being engrossed in this detested source, Byrnes makes a statement which Mr. Minns reflected was planed to irritate Mr. Davis.

He did not learn the first part, but it finished with "killing the negroes." Mr. Davis sensed that it was planed as a taunt to him. He replied him, "Then, on that belief, you must to have your throats cut." I have no suspect it was a lucid resolution from Mr. Byrnes' property, and none more.

Up to this purpose, what is the established items against Mr. Davis? Am I not right in saying, none whatever--nothing more than any man would be subject to, who carried on as counsel?
