He was always a gentleman and a man of the world; he did not dislike mixing with
men of all classes and all parties; he had none of that stiffness and hauteur which many of
his friends had acquired from their military pursuits. His relations with his opponents are
illustrated by an anecdote of which there are many versions. He found himself one day while
in the refreshment room standing side by side with d'Ester, one of the most extreme of the
Republican party.
