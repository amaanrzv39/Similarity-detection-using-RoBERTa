The great resources of Illinois were shown in every department of the exposition

That which held the most commanding and was  pretentious of all the State buildings is the State House. Though not on the Plateau of States, it held a generous verandas and a wealth of ornament. Sitting west of the Cascade Gardens, across from the gardens of Japan and directly south of the Lincoln Museum and was an important part of another State group on the Trail.

Though modern in treatment , the State House was designed in the French Renaissance times.
