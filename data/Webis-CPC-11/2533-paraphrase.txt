The result may be easily guessed. For two days the young people were left, naturally, very much together. They quickly fell in to an easy intimacy, and on the third and last day of the holiday Angelo was profoundly in love. Gone were the botanizers, gone the bibliomants, gone the Deputy Harbour Masters. There was but two thought in his evacuated brain, to make the fair Sylvia his own.
His opportunity came after dinner that night when the rest of the party had gone out to look at some condemned pheasants which were to be shot at dawn. They was at the piano playing that deservedly popular song, "I've chipped my chip for England," by Nathaniel Dayer, when they suddenly leant over her.
