
CHAPTER II--FRANCIE

Francie was eleven years old, shy, secret, and rather childish of his age, though not backward
in schooling, which had been pushed on far by a private governor, one M'Brair, a forfeited
minister harboured in that capacity at Montroymont.  The boy, already much employed in secret
by his mother, was the most apt hand conceivable to run upon a message, to carry food to lurking
fugitives, or to stand sentry on the skyline above a conventicle.
