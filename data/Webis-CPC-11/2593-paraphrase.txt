Merill B Sherwood, Jr a resident of Buffalo, N.Y obtained a patent for a phosphorescent composition on 9th of August, 1881.

He made some improvements in phosphorescent illuminants by taking the advantage of peculiar property which obtains in many bodies i:e of absorbing light during the day and emitting it during the night time.

Mr. Sherwood says that when applied with oil or varnish, his improved luminous substance can be exposed to the same weather conditions as an ordinay paint is exposed to. According to him the substance will not suffer any kind of diminution in its luminous property. 

Mr. Sherwood's luminous substance is composed of calcined chalk, sulphur and bismuth substantially.
