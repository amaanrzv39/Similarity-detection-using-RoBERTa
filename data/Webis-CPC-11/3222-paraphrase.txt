The second requirement--that of securing "a disciplined mind." Here again it is essential to commence to instil the principles which form a disciplined mind as early as possible in the boy's early youth. Self-denial, obedience to the orders of their superiors, & respect & affection for their elders, are perhaps the most important of these principles.

This task may again be safely left to the officers of the Education Department.

It will be seen, therefore, that the first one requirements may be obtained by a technique, as advocated above, to be imparted to all boys in their early youth by those who are charged with their simple education, & it is urged that such technique should be uniform & form part of the school curriculum, the teachers being necessary to qualify to impart the necessary instruction.
