Gordon's mission, according to Earl Granville, was a quiet four, and they inquired anxiously what progress had been made in the withdrawal of the Egyptian garrisons and civilians. This query they put, even in the teeth of Gordon's positive statement in a telegram of March 8:--
If you do not send Zebehr, you have no chance of getting the garrisons away; . . . Zebehr here would be far more powerful than the Mahdi, and they would make short work of the Mahdi[394].

I will do my best to carryover out my instructions, but I feel conviction I shall be caught in Khartum[395].
