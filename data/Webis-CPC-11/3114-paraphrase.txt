As before long as he has got what he wishes for, and taken from me, what's now my only Boast my Maiden-head, my Honour and his Love will both be lost together: and then I will have none left me but too late Repentance. This so effectually wrought upon him, That he made all the Protestations in the World, Nothing shou'd ever part em, if she'd but condescend to accept of him for a Gallant: For tho he had a Wife, 'twas one he cou'd not love, and didn't nurture her; where he observed those Charms in her, that would constrain him to be perpetually constant. And that if she would agree to be as accurate to him as she shou'd perpetually find him accurate to her, it wou'd be all the cheerfulness he'd ask.--And now, to make the Bargain firm, the Bawd retains services for both Parties, that they will each be accurate to one another.
