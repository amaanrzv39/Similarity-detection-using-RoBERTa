At six thiry-five in the morning, with the route being that of north to north to east, there was no changes noted. At eleven twenty in the morning, we came to a stop to give the horses a rest. Then at one forty in the afternoon, we started back again. At three forty in the afternoon, we reached a massive group of natives near a body of water. Five of them followed us for awhile. They they were not satisfied until we decided to make an exchange of items, which we did as a token of peace.
