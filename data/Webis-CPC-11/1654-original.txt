He was very kind. He laid all the blame on himself
for expecting that the method of making marriages by arrangement would be a success where a
youthful Britisher was concerned. He, however, wished I should tell him all that had happened
since he had seen me at supper, and especially about my meeting with Gustave.

I just told him--as I have told you, pointing out that the affair had been quite harmless,
though appearances were certainly against me. He left the house and returned later on.
