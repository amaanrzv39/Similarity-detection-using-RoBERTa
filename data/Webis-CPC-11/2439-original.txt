
THE BROOKLYN CASE.

For several years past there have been rumors more or less definite in character that a young
lady in Brooklyn was not only living without food, but was possessed of some mysterious faculty
by which she could foretell events, read communications without the aid of the eyes, and accurately
describe occurrences in distant places, through clairvoyance or whatever other name may be
applied to the influence.

Finally, in the New York Herald of October 20th, 1878, appeared an account, headed "Life without
Food.
