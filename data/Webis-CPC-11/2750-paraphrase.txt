An aged settler who came with his family advised me "Our total outfit comprised a quill bed and a luncheon basket in which were a cutting implement, split and two tiny chinaware dishes. I in addition paid for a solitary mattress and a couple of woollen shells in Cleveland on my way to Duluth. We assembled our bedsteads out of green tamarack rods peeled, employing the bark for lines to retain it concurrently and made a table of two slabs which were encountered balancing in the Bay. Bed clothes contained of Indian woollen shells and moccasins replied for boots, while curtains, carpets and upholstered furnishings were unknown."

The postoffice was in a tiny erected structure on First Street and First Avenue East. The postmaster, Mr. Richard Marvin was a component of the Fire Brigade.
