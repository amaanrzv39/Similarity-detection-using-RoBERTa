
He would adorn his hut with fragrant boughs, and as he fed and caressed his kids, would sing
with a light heart the songs of old Scotland. Then at set of sun he returned to the hut in
which he slept, and there once more sang, and read, and prayed, and so lay down to sleep in
peace, because he knew that it was the Lord only that made him dwell in safety.

"I was a better Christian in my solitude than ever I was before, or than I fear I shall ever
be again," he said, years after he had left the island.
