It is not important to trace the place through all its owners. In the reign of Henry VIII. it was the property of Thomas Howard, the first Duke of Norfolk. On the request of his son, the castle given to the crown. Soon, it was granted to Sir John Byron for fifty years. In the reign of James I., Gilbert Talbot, Earl of Shrewsbury, was the owner of Bolsover. In the year 1613, he sold it to Sir Charles Cavendish, whose eldest son William, was the first Duke of Newcastle, a personage of great eminence among the nobility of his time, and in high favour at court.[1] He was sincerely attached to his royal master, Charles I., whom he entertained at Bolsover Castle thrice, in a style of regal splendor.
