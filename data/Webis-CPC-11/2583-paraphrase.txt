Broad, manly views & hopeful thoughts of life exist less here, they think, than in England. The cities are supplied year by year with people from the country; yet the latter, the source of all this supply, does not produce so healthy mothers as the city; & were it not for the increasing study of physiology & its vital truths, they fear that they ought to awaken late to a knowledge of our physical degeneration.
Now what means are in use among us to furnish the needed stimulant of exercise? It is paradoxical to say that the average of people take more exercise in the city than in the country; yet they believe it to be true. That exercise is only of five form, to be sure, namely, walking.
