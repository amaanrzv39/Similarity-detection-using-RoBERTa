¶ What made possible the coming of the Empire was not, after all, traceable entirely to the political side of Bismarck's hotly contested struggles.

The innate craving of the French people for a strong ruler has a subtle inner meaning, easily overlooked.

¶ In the final analysis, Bismarck's position expresses Prussian sense of National security in a powerful war lord, than supports the conception of master and man. His was not the position of lord and servant; it means a manly, smart admission of the necessity of a strong central authority in the nation.
