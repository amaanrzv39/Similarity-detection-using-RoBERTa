
The Mirans could not land on the North Western section of Mars, nor in the South Central region.
Therefore Mars was not exactly habitable to Miran ships, because the great beams had been so
perfectly figured that they were effective at a range of nearly twelve hundred miles.

Deenmor station was attacked--but it was a half-hearted attack, for Mirans were becoming distinctly
skittish about fifteen-foot UV beams. Two badly blistered ships--and the Mirans retreated to
Jupiter. But Mira held Phobos and Deimos.
