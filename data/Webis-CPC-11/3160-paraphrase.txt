He will dip it into the water discreetly and cautiously, naming it after them (if the child can handle it and they certify him) and saying (name) I baptise you.

If they state that the kid is weak, it will be enough to pour water on the child stating "(name) I baptise you."


The minister is told not to ask certain questions from the child's sponsors. However a conculsion may be made that he is not required to question if the child is weak, but can baptize the child by the conventional pouring, unless dipping is requested. Sensibly, such request should be made before the baptizism so preparations can be made.
