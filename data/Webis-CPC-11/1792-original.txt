But the mind was as clearly the result
of the bodily organization as the music of the harpsichord is the result of the instrumental
mechanism.  The mind shared the decrepitude of the body in extreme old age, and in the full
vigour of youth a sudden injury to the brain might forever destroy the intellect of a Plato
or a Shakspeare.  But the third principle,--the soul,--the something lodged within the body,
which yet was to survive it? Where was that soul hidden out of the ken of the anatomist?
