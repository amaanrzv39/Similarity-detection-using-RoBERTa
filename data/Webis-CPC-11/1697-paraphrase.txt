A third problem needed to be cleared up before all could be considered settled between all nations concerned with fishing the great Banks adjacent to the United Kingdom.  Although the waters of the Banks are considered open sea and fishing is permitted for all, Great Britain retained  sovereignty over coastal waters and her own ports and there were no rights guaranteed any other nations for usage thereof.  The Treaty of 1783 was null and void unless an agreement could be struck on this matter between contesting nations.
