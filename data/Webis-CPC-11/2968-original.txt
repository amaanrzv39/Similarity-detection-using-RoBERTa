[Sidenote: Zeno introduces a new method.]

Zeno of Elea, the friend and pupil of Parmenides, born B.C. 500, brought nothing new to the
system, but invented Dialectics, that logic which afterwards became so powerful in the hands
of Plato and Aristotle, and so generally admired among the schoolmen. It seeks to establish
truth by refuting error by the reductio ad absurdum.
