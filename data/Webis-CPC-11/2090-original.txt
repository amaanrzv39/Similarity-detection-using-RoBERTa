The story of their terrible journey is unwritten, but it is known
that many died of slow starvation and fatigue along the route, which led through swamps and
thickets, with deep rivers barring their path; and not until the last of December did they
reach the forts, after having been twelve weeks in the wilderness. The number of those who
perished in the wreck or died on the journey is not recorded, but it was so large as to occasion
petitions to the government--an unusual proceeding at that early date.
