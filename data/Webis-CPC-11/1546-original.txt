The Parisians dreaded the vengeance of these aristocrats who were like to poison them with
their dead bodies.

Évariste Gamelin joined the line; he was resolved to spare his old mother the fatigues of the
long wait. His neighbour, the citoyen Brotteaux, went with him, calm and smiling, his Lucretius
in the baggy pocket of his plum-coloured coat.
