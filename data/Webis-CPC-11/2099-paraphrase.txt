He took a break for sequel and respiration.

"Now you 'ave' 'ad a look at me you will grasp me. Not the Apollo Belgravia, but just simple Brown-- Mrs. Brown's old man--yes, its me; and due to 'Eaven it's 'im you have got to cope with and not Mr. Brown's old lady. Now we will get to toil. fellers, and 'ustle' the word.

He stepped away a few distance.

"When I express 'Round me nip,'' he yelled, "I want to see a altocumulus of dust and a living statue. Cycle me--NIP!"

There was fisticuffs.

"'It 'im, "shouted Brown; "you are not doing a foxtrot!
