Every one was habitually on the qui vive for a vein of gold or shiny, but no part of New France ever provided the smallest sign of an El Dorado. Prospecting committed the powers of numerous colonists in every lifetime, but most of those who therefore expended their years at it got not anything but a generous bonus of chagrin.

Mention should furthermore be made of the brewing commerce which Talon set upon its feet throughout his short intendancy but which, like all remainder of his designs, did not long endure his departure. In setting up a brewery at Quebec the paternal intendant had two finishes in mind: first, to decrease the large utilisation of eau-de-vie by supplying a lower and more healthy substitute; and second, to furnish the ranchers of the colony with a money-making dwelling market for their grain.
