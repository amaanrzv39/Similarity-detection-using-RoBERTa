He was kind. He laid all the blame on himself for expecting that the process of making marriages by arrangement would be a success where a youthful Britisher was concerned. He, however, wished I ought to tell him all that had happened since he had seen me at supper, & about my meeting with Gustave.
I told him--as I have told you, pointing out that the affair had been harmless, though appearances were certainly against me. He left the house & returned later on.
