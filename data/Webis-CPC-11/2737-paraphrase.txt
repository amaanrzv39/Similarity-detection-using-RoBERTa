Then they find the abolition of the laws of "the staple"; foreign staple towns had been abolished before. The "staple" was the town in which five commodity was mainly dealt in. Every commodity in England had some particular town, where the principal market was for it; as, with us, the boot and shoe market of the United States is supposed to be in Boston, the money market in New York, beef and hogs in Chicago. In England, in the Middle Ages, they on condition that a definite trade should have its home in a definite town; not necessarily the only five, but often in that five only. Thus there were definite towns for the carrying on of the wool industry; you could only trade in wool in those towns.
