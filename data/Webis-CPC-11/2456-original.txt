Thus far we
may credit him-- but what man of common sense can believe, that Richard went so far as publicly
to asperse the honor of his own mother? That mother, Cecily duchess dowager of York, a princess
of a spotless character, was then living: so were two of her daughters, the duchesses of Suffolk
and Burgundy, Richard's own sisters: one of them, the duchess of Suffolk walked at his ensuing
coronation, and her son the earl of Lincoln was by Richard himself, after the death of his
own son, declared heir apparent to the crown.
