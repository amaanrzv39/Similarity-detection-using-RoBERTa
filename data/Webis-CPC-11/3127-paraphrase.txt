Although he had faith in her, he could not imagine what occurred to prevent her from answering. The most obvious answer --that the letter had been lost-- did not enter his mind. The potential for small creatures to ignite major upheaval, is not something generally considered. Even if he had her address, this would not cure his impasse. He would not hound her with love. On top of his suffering over her, he was riddled with anxiety that taxed his sanity. Not knowing why she suddenly left for Germany left him with dark struggles to find the meaning of her departure. Sometimes his reasoning rested on the possibility that she suddenly felt compelled to fulfill her noble obligation. Perhaps she married the other man and went to Germany with him.
