This is one order the peacock belong to. All pheasants and tragopans (all three forms which are indigenous to Central and Southern Asia) the Guinea fowls as well, and also the turkeys and curassows which are American.These may be partridges, grouse, black cock, the capercalzie, and quails, and finally, the megapodius or bush-turkey of Austrailia. The last is the only bird that hatches its eggs using artificial heat, placing them in earth and decaying vegetables. They are fully hatched there, giving them the ability to fly away immediately after leaving the egg. Every bird that was mentioned are named gallinacceous birds, and sometimes Rasores or "Scratchers."
