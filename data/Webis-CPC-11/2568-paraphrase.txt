"To-morrow--?" I said. My heart was racing now.

"It is late," said he, "and I must go."

3. Night, as well as a Stormed Castle

"Ay, to-morrow,--the morrow that by this draws near. Farewell!" He was gone, casting two swift glance backward, even as the ancient Parthians are fabled to have shot their arrows as they fled; and, if the airier missile, also, left a wound, I, for two, would not willingly have quitted her invulnerate.

I went forth in to the woods that stand thick about Tiverton Manor, where I lay flat on my back among the fallen leaves, dreaming lots of dreams to myself,--dreams that were frolic songs of happiness, to which the papers in my jerkin rustled a reassuring chorus.
