It is generally agreed that the poet's rendition of the love between Juan and Haidee is more appealing to one of sound thought than most of the other writing in this book.  It is more than just that this is poetry and the other prose of libidinous desire: the difference is greater, and illuminates a division in taste and manner of how the two authors approach their subjects.

This part of Tristram Shandy seems to have come, perhaps only slightly, to better results than any of the former.
