
He had often heard his grandfather descant on his English ancestors, and his wealthy connexions
in the old country; it struck him, therefore, while thus hanging loosely on society, that it
might be no unwise thing to visit these relatives, and claim alliance with them. With this
view he proceeded to New York, and made his terms with the master of a vessel bound for Plymouth.
Here he was set down, without money, without friends, or even a single acquaintance.
