
"Take the three. I'll send them home at once. Where does your honour live? Boy, a cord!"

"Not so fast, my friend," cried the artist, startled from his reverie, and perceiving the brisk
dealer about to tie up the three daubs. His first impulse was to walk away, but he felt ashamed
to purchase nothing after standing so long before the shop, and causing the hungry-looking
old salesman so large an expenditure of breath. "Wait a little," he said. "I will see if you
have any thing to suit me." And, stooping down, he turned over a number of battered dusty old
pictures heaped like lumber upon the ground. They were chiefly old-fashioned family portraits,
likenesses of unknown and insignificant faces, with torn canvass, and frames that had lost
their gilding.
