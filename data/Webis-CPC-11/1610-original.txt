
In acknowledgment of such attentions, Florence always insisted that before the close of his
engagements we should all be his guests at a regular Italian luncheon of spaghetti at Caproni's,
down on Wabash Avenue. It is needless to say that the spaghetti was merely the central dish,
around which revolved and was devoured every delicacy that Florence had ever heard of in his
Italian itinerary, the whole washed down with strange wines from the same sunny land.
