What makes his journal so divergent from other journals on floating in the air is that in it we have a novice distress from all types of misfortunes and faults before he has mastered the annoyances of his art. Whether deliberately or not Captain HALL puts on a very many service in delineating the life of a flier while his wings are--so to speak--only in the sprouting stage. In an introduction Major GROS advises us of the work wrapped up by American pilots before America moved into the War, a very gratifying preface to a journal which both for its subject and approach is good to read.
