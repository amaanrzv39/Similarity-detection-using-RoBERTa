Being faithful to their legislative declaration the only knew but one issue, that being slavery. Every difference, failure to comply, every hesitation, and even all silence, was labeled as "abolitionism," in every town, as in Leavenworth, and stated to be hostile to the well being of the public, and punished with physical violence, suspension, prohibition, and often death. It would be nearly impossible to dictate the horrific details of the killings, hangings, and mobs.
