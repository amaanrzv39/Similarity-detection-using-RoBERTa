It is impossible to make every boy an artist
or a connoisseur, but quite possible to make him understand the meaning of art in its rudiments,
and to make him modest enough to forbear expressing, in after life, judgments which he has
not knowledge enough to render just.

157. There is, however, at present this great difficulty in the way of such systematic teaching--that
the public do not believe the principles of art are determinable, and, in no wise, matters
of opinion.
