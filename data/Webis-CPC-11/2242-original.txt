In 1854, at the Metropolitan Free Hospital, situated in the Jews' quarter in London, Hutchinson
observed that the proportion of Jews to Christians among the out-patients was as one to three;
at the same time the proportion of cases of syphilis in the former to the latter was one to
fifteen. Now, this result was not due to any extra morality on the part of the Jews, as fully
one-half of the gonorrhoea cases occurred among those of that faith.
