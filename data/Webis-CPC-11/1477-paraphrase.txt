A laugh raised at the expense of a brother is enough to justify the severest slash.  Comparative Philology, which owes its existence and progress to the labors of German scholars, and whose first representative, Bopp, is still living and teaching in Berlin, is more and more pursed of late.  Sanscrit is now taught uniersally; and lectures are delivered on the affinities of the Indo-Germanic languages with each other and with the mother-tongue of all.
