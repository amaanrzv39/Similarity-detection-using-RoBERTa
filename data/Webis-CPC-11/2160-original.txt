
As his mind became more peaceful, he turned his thoughts to the question of a shelter from
the storms of the approaching winter, which, even in that mild climate, was often accompanied
with frost and snow. There were plenty of trees on the island, and with their stems and branches
he soon built for himself a rough hut, which he thatched with long grass cut and dried in the
sun. This attempt was so successful that he determined to build another hut at a short distance,
so that he might sleep in one, and in the other, prepare his food.
