
But we have, in addition to all these districts of Alleghania, a vast reserve in Texas--that
Texas which is now more than half cultivated by free labor, and which is amply capable of producing
six times as much cotton as is now raised in the entire South. An armed occupation of Texas,
a copious stream of emigration thither, to be encouraged by very liberal grants to settlers,
and a speedy completion of its railroads, would be an offset to secession, well worth of itself
all that the war has cost.
