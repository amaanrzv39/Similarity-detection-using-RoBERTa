"Aged thirty years," again interrupted the Cure. "Henri Rouget, idiot; as young as the morning. For man grows elderly only by what they suffers, and what they forgives, and what they sins. What have you to say for Henri Rouget, my Francois?"

"Chief John has lost his right hand. Go on."

The priest rose to his feet and put a hand on the young man's shoulder.

"Do you know, Francois," they said, half sadly, "do you know, you have the true thing in you. Come often to me, my son, and bring all these things --all you write."
