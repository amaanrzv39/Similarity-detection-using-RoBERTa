The prevailing fashion of certain orators interlarding their speeches with common classical quotations, reminds us of a piece of naughty wiggery perpetrated by one of the greatest men of his time. Sheridan once electrified the country gentlemen in the House of Commons, by closing an animated appeal to their patriotism, with a quote from Herodotus, which they cheered most vociferously; when, in fact, he merely strung together a jumble of words, a jargon uttered on the instant, which sound very much like Greek.
