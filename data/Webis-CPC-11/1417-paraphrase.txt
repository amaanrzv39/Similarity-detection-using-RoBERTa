What evidence is there that the tempest posesses magical qualities? When Prospero has the passengers and crew of the ship, what does he gain? Does he do it to get revenge on his foes? Or perhaps to exploit the relationship of Ferdinand and Miranda. Maybe even a vain attempt to have himself reinstated in good faith. What does he gain by this love? The reader cannot help but to be naturally immersed into the past of Prospero and Miranda, given specific bits of necessary information.
