Travelers going from Williamsburg to Richmond were given a heck of a party. With the start of the new government, people started dropping by the Fitzhugs' place so often, they were being eaten out of house and home. They really needed to get away from all the moochers, so they decided to buy a house in Alexandria, and in 1796, they found a realtor to sell the house in Chatham. The Fitzhugs were friends with George Washington, and the two families were real close and tight with each other.
