M. Alexandre had his head crushed, at the crossroads of the Rue Gambetta and the Rue Carnot. Germans entered the store of M. Brèche, wine seller. Thinking, no doubt, that he was not helping them fast enough, they dragged his body into the courtyard of Mme. Egasse, his next door neighbor, where an officer blamed him of having fired on the soldiers, and ordered that he should be shot at once. Mme. Egasse tried to soften the hearts of the murderers, but she was brutally ordered off. From the room to which she went, she heard the noise, and through the window she saw Brèche's body stretched on the ground. When she came down she could not prevent herself from expressing her grief. The officer then said to her: "A dead man!
