He announced that his command would devolve on General Grant, who
had been summoned around from Memphis to Corinth by way of Columbus, Kentucky, and that I was
to go into Memphis to take command of the District of West Tennessee, vacated by General Grant.
By this time, also, I was made aware that the great, army that had assembled at Corinth at
the end of May had been scattered and dissipated, and that terrible disasters had befallen
our other armies in Virginia and the East.
