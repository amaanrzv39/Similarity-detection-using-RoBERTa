Indeed, it had long had exponents elsewhere. Thus in England, a full one 100 beforehand, Dr. Hartley had championed the model of the close and indissoluble dependence of the psyche upon the head, and formulated a renowned vibration model of association that still warrants painstaking consideration. Then, too, in France, at the commencing of the one 100, there was Dr. Cabanis with his real, if basically phrased, doctrine that the head digests appearances and secretes reflected as the stomach digests sustenance and the liver secretes bile. Moreover, Herbert Spencer's Principles of Psychology, with its avowed co-ordination of psyche and body and its vitalizing model of evolution, seemed in 1855, half a period of 10 years before the work of Fechner.
