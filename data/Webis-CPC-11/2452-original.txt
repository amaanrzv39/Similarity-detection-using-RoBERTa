Let the reader try for some conception of its environment and it, as the floor or arena
of a great transaction this day.

Daun stands fronting southward along these Siptitz Heights, looking towards Schilda and his
dangerous neighbor; heights, woods, ponds and inaccessibilities environing his Position and
him. One of the strongest positions imaginable; which, under Prince Henri, proved inexpugnable
enough to some of us. A position not to be attacked on that southern front, nor on either of
its flanks:--where can it be attacked?
