It is quite possible, of course, to appreciate, pleasantly and externally, the Iliad with its force of thronging life and its daring unity, and the Odyssey with its serener life and its superb construction, though much more sectional unity. But we do not be grateful for what Homer did for his time, and is still doing for all the world, we do not appreciate the spirit of his music, unless we see the warfare and the escapade as symbols of the primary courage of life; and there is additional in those words than seems when they are baldly written.
