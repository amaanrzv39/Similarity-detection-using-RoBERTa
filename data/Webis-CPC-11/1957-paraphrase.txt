Faithful to their legislative declaration they knew but one issue, slavery.  All dissent, all non-compliance, all hesitation, all were silence even, were in their stronghold towns, like Leavenworth, branded as "abolitionism," declared to be hostility to the public welfare, and punished with proscription, personal violence, expulsion, and frequenctly death.  Of the lynchings, themobs, and the murders, it would be impossibe, except in a very extended work, to note the frequent and atrocious details.
