After they had entered under the stone roof where they could
not only sit comfortably, but also stand and walk about she seated herself close to him and
kept very quiet.

"Mother will not be angry," said Conrad, "we shall tell her of the heavy snow that has kept
us, and she will say nothing; father will not, either. And if we grow cold, why then we must
slap our hands to our bodies as the woodcutters did, and then we shall grow warm again."

"Yes, Conrad," said the girl.
