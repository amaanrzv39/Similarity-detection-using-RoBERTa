Wherever
the Romans turned their eyes they saw nothing but menacing reminders of surrender and defeat.
They must not be alarmed by that sudden change of fortune in the battle at Trier. It was their
own victory which hampered the Germans there: they had dropped their weapons and filled their
hands with loot. Since then everything had gone in their favour and against the Romans. He
had taken every possible precaution, as befitted a cunning general.
