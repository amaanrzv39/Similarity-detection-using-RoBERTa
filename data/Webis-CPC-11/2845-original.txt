They had
much to say about the duty of children, and very little about the love of parents. The sacrificing
of children to idols, a not uncommon practice in some renowned countries of antiquity, the
highest-born children being the favorite victims,--for Moloch's appetite was delicate,--could
never have taken place in any country where the voice of Nature was heeded; and yet those sacrifices
were but so many proofs of the existence of a spirit of pride, which caused men to offer up
their offspring on the domestic altar. Son and slave were almost the same word with the Romans;
and your genuine old Roman made little ado about cutting off the head of one of his boys, perhaps
for doing something of a praiseworthy nature.
