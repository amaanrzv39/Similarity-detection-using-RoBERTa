Far lower in its sphere than that infallible
inspiration which speaks to us through the sacred pages of Holy Writ of the things immediately
pertaining to our relations with God, true artistic power must still be considered as inspiration,
since it is constantly arriving at more than the unassisted reason of man could command by
the fullest exercise of its highest logical powers. The impassioned Romeo cries: 'Can philosophy
make a Juliet?' That philosophy has never made a Juliet in art is positively certain!
